Leo Tolstoy (1828-1910)

A Russian novelist celebrated for masterpieces like "War and Peace" and "Anna Karenina," Tolstoy's works delve deep into human nature and society.

Jane Austen (1775-1817)

An English novelist known for her keen observations of the British landed gentry, Austen's classics include "Pride and Prejudice" and "Sense and Sensibility."

Gabriel García Márquez (1927-2014)

Gabriel García Márquez was a Colombian novelist, short-story writer, and journalist, best known for pioneering the literary style of Magical Realism. He won the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1982 for his contributions to literature. García Márquez’s works remain widely read and studied, continuing to captivate readers with their rich narratives and deep emotional impact.

Agatha Christie (1890-1976)
